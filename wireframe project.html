Traditional ukrainian embroidery

HISTORY


Embroidery is an ancient and symbolic tradition in Ukraine. Excavations of cities from the 1st century AD have revealed examples of embroidered clothing in the territory of Ukraine. Embroidery was mostly used for the decoration of clothing and fabrics and for the decoration of homes and churches. Embroidered products, especially a rushnyk, are greatly symbolic for a series of ceremonies and rituals of Ukraine. Most embroidery was used for clothing. The primary object of clothing that was decorated with embroidery was the shirt or vyshyvanka.

ORNAMENTS


The designs have a long history which defines its ornamental motifs and compositions, as well as its favorite choice of colors and types of stitches. Folk embroidery was symbolic and connected with a great number of beliefs, myths, and superstitions, including beliefs regarding protection and fertility. Red and black were the most common colors of Ukrainian embroidery. The lozenge shape is a common motif and represents a sown field and female fertility.

PATTERNS


Ukrainian embroidery has many variations from region to region, or even village to village.However, most embroidery is generally similar overall for most Ukrainians. Even with these variation, the styles of needlework found throughout Ukraine, when taken together, represent a definite Ukrainian national style of embroidery. Ukrainian folk embroidery is notable for great variety of its techniques.
